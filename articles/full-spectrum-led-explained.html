Full Spectrum LED — What Does It Actually Mean?

5 min read · July 2026

If you've shopped for grow lights in the last few years, you've seen "Full Spectrum" plastered on every product page. But what does it actually mean? And does it matter for your grow?

The Short Answer

Full spectrum means the light emits wavelengths across the entire PAR range (400–700nm) — mimicking natural sunlight. This includes blue, green, red, and far-red light in proportions that plants can use throughout their entire life cycle.

Blurple vs Full Spectrum

WavelengthsMostly red + blueFull PAR range (400–700nm)

Your eyesHard to see plant healthNatural color — easy to inspect

Why Full Spectrum Wins

  • Better efficiency: Modern full-spectrum LEDs convert more electricity into usable photons. The best lights in our dataset hit 2.7+ µmol/J — nearly double what blurple lights achieve.
  • One light, all stages: No need to swap lights between veg and flower. Full spectrum covers both.
  • Green light matters: Blurple lights skip green wavelengths, but research shows green light penetrates deeper into the canopy, reaching lower leaves that red/blue can't.
  • Easier plant inspection: Under white light, you can actually see leaf color, spot deficiencies, and catch pests. Under blurple, everything looks purple.

What About "Enhanced" Spectrums?

Some manufacturers offer spectrum variants:

  • R-Spec (Red enhanced): Extra 660nm deep red for flowering. Slightly better bud development.
  • UV/IR enhanced: Adds ultraviolet or infrared diodes. Claims of increased trichome production — research is mixed but promising.
  • Veg-specific: Higher blue ratio for compact vegetative growth. Useful but not necessary with a dimmable full-spectrum light.

💡 Bottom line: For most growers, a standard full-spectrum LED is all you need. The efficiency gains over blurple lights pay for themselves in electricity savings within 1–2 grows. Use our calculator to see the exact savings.

Is It Worth Upgrading?

If you're running a blurple light from 2018 or earlier, yes. The efficiency difference alone means you'll:

  • Get 50–80% more light for the same electricity
  • Run cooler (less heat = less AC/ventilation needed)
  • See better yields from more complete spectrum coverage
  • Save money every single grow cycle on your electric bill
