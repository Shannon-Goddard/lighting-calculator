How Much Wattage Do I Need Per Square Foot?

6 min read · July 2026

You've got your tent. Now you need to figure out how much light to fill it. The internet is full of conflicting advice — 30 watts per square foot, 50 watts, "it depends on efficacy." Let's cut through it.

The Rule of Thumb

Growth StageTarget W/sqftNotes
Veg only20–30 W/sqftLess intensity needed for leaf growth
Flowering30–50 W/sqftMore light = denser buds
High performance50+ W/sqftWith CO2 supplementation

What This Looks Like by Tent Size

TentSq FtVeg (min)Flower (target)
2×2480W120–200W
3×39180W270–450W
4×416320W480–650W
5×525500W750–1000W

These numbers assume modern LED efficiency (2.0+ µmol/J). Older or cheaper LEDs with lower efficacy may need more wattage to hit the same PPFD targets.

Why Efficacy Matters More Than Raw Watts

A 600W light with 2.7 µmol/J efficacy produces significantly more usable light than a 600W light at 1.8 µmol/J. The first light delivers ~1,620 µmol/s of photons. The second delivers ~1,080 µmol/s. Same electricity bill, 50% more light.

This is why "watts per square foot" is a starting point, not the final answer. Check the efficacy rating when comparing lights — our comparison tool shows this side-by-side.

💡 Quick formula: Watts needed = tent length × tent width × 35
This gives you a solid middle-ground target for flowering with a modern LED.

Don't Forget the Electricity Cost

More watts = more electricity. A 600W light running 12 hours/day in flower costs roughly $1.15/day at the national average rate ($0.16/kWh). Over an 8-week flower cycle, that's $64 in electricity alone.

The Bottom Line

  • Target 30–50 W/sqft for flowering with modern LEDs
  • Check efficacy — higher µmol/J means more light per watt
  • Match your light to your tent — use the "Browse by Tent Size" feature on our calculator page to find lights that fit
  • Factor in electricity — the cheapest light isn't always the cheapest to run
